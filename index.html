M&M's are colorful button-shaped chocolates produced by Mars, Incorporated. The candy shell, each of which has the letter "m" printed in lower case on one side, surrounds a filling which varies depending upon the variety of M&M's. M&M's are available in various colors and flavors, making them a popular choice among candy lovers of all ages.

The history of M&M's dates back to 1941 when Forrest Mars Sr., the son of the founder of the Mars Company, struck a partnership with Bruce Murrie, the son of the Hershey Chocolate president. The two combined their resources to produce the beloved candy that has since become a household name.

Over the years, M&M's has introduced various flavors and limited-edition colors to celebrate different holidays and events. Some of the popular flavors include peanut, peanut butter, almond, and crispy. The company has also expanded its product line to include M&M's Minis, M&M's Mega, and even personalized M&M's with custom prints.

M&M's are not just a tasty treat; they have also become an iconic brand known for its clever advertising campaigns and memorable characters, such as the spokescandies Red, Yellow, Blue, and Green. These characters have been featured in commercials, print ads, and even animated series, further cementing the brand's popularity and recognition worldwide.

In addition to their regular candy products, M&M's has ventured into other avenues, including limited-edition collaborations with other brands and themed merchandise. The brand continues to innovate and captivate audiences with its unique and creative marketing strategies, ensuring its place as a beloved candy staple for generations to come.

About Us

M&M's is a world-renowned brand of chocolate candies that has been delighting consumers since 1941. The colorful button-shaped candies are produced by Mars, Incorporated, and are known for their vibrant colors, variety of flavors, and clever advertising campaigns.

The history of M&M's began when Forrest Mars Sr., the son of the founder of the Mars Company, partnered with Bruce Murrie, the son of the Hershey Chocolate president. Together, they created the iconic M&M's candy, which quickly became a popular and beloved treat enjoyed by people of all ages around the world.

Over the years, M&M's has expanded its product line to include a wide variety of flavors and limited-edition colors, celebrating different holidays and events. Some of the popular flavors include peanut, peanut butter, almond, crispy, and many more. The company has also introduced M&M's Minis, M&M's Mega, and personalized M&M's with custom prints, offering consumers a unique and personalized candy experience.

M&M's is not just a tasty treat; it is also an iconic brand known for its innovative and creative marketing strategies. The brand's memorable characters, such as the spokescandies Red, Yellow, Blue, and Green, have become cultural icons and have been featured in commercials, print ads, and even animated series, further cementing the brand's popularity and recognition worldwide.

Today, M&M's continues to captivate audiences with its unique and creative marketing campaigns, limited-edition collaborations with other brands, and themed merchandise. With its rich history, innovative products, and iconic branding, M&M's remains a beloved candy staple for generations to come.
